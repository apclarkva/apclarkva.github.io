About me

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the University of Virginia Department of Biomedical Engineering with Dr. Jeffrey Saucerman. My work integrates experimental measurements with computational models to decode how transcriptional programs shape cardiac development and disease.

Previously, I earned my Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering at Cornell University with Dr. David Christini, where I developed single-cell phenotyping approaches to explain heterogeneity in stem cell-derived cardiomyocytes and uncover mechanisms of arrhythmia.

Recent highlights

Selected milestones from my current and recent work.

Gene regulatory networks in development

Modeling segment-specific regulatory circuits to explain how cardiomyocytes transition between chamber identities during heart tube morphogenesis.

Single-cell electrophysiology

Built computational pipelines that pair automated voltage clamp with mechanistic models to understand drug-induced arrhythmia risk.

Modeling platforms

Developed logic-based network tools and open workflows to make systems biology modeling accessible to experimental collaborators.
